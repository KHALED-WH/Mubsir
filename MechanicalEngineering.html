Brief

Mechanical Engineering is a versatile and fundamental field of engineering that involves the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. It prepares students to create and innovate machinery and devices used in various industries.

Key Areas of Study

Thermodynamics

Study of energy, heat transfer, and conversion of energy in mechanical systems.

Fluid Mechanics

Analysis of the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) and their interactions.

Skills Developed

  • Strong foundation in mathematics and physics
  • Proficiency in computer-aided design (CAD) tools
  • Mechanical design and manufacturing skills

Career Opportunities

Mechanical Engineer

Designs, develops, and tests mechanical systems and components.

Automotive Engineer

Designs and tests vehicles and their components focusing on efficiency.

Benefits of the Major

  • Broad skill set applicable to many industries.
  • Involvement in addressing complex engineering challenges.
