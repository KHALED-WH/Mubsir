Brief

Cyber Security is a crucial field focused on protecting computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats and attacks. This discipline equips students with the knowledge and skills to defend against unauthorized access, data breaches, and other cyber incidents. Graduates are prepared to work in various industries, ensuring the security and integrity of digital information and infrastructure.

Key Areas of Study

Network Security

Strategies and techniques to protect computer networks from intrusions and attacks, ensuring data integrity and availability.

Cryptography

Study of techniques for securing information through encryption and decryption, ensuring data confidentiality and authenticity.

Ethical Hacking

Techniques used by security professionals to identify and fix security vulnerabilities by simulating cyber attacks.

Incident Response

Procedures and strategies to detect, respond to, and recover from cyber incidents and breaches.

Security Governance

Development and implementation of policies, standards, and frameworks to manage and reduce cybersecurity risks.

Risk Management

Assessment and mitigation of risks associated with cyber threats, ensuring business continuity and data protection.

Digital Forensics

Techniques for investigating cyber crimes and analyzing digital evidence to support legal proceedings.

Skills Developed

  • Proficiency in cybersecurity tools and techniques
  • Strong understanding of network and system security principles
  • Knowledge of cryptography and encryption methods
  • Ability to conduct vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
  • Incident response and management skills
  • Analytical and problem-solving abilities
  • Strong communication and teamwork skills

Career Opportunities

Graduates in Cyber Security can pursue careers in various sectors, including finance, healthcare, government, and technology. Common job roles include:

Cyber Security Analyst

Monitors and protects an organization's IT infrastructure from cyber threats, ensuring data security and compliance.

Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)

Conducts simulated cyber attacks to identify and fix security vulnerabilities in systems and networks.

Security Consultant

Advises organizations on best practices and strategies for improving their cybersecurity posture.

Incident Response Specialist

Handles and mitigates cyber incidents, developing strategies to prevent future breaches.

Security Architect

Designs and implements secure IT systems and networks to protect against cyber threats.

Digital Forensics Investigator

Investigates cyber crimes, collecting and analyzing digital evidence for legal and security purposes.

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

Oversees an organization's cybersecurity strategy, ensuring the protection of data and IT assets.

Benefits of the Major

  • High Demand: Increasing need for skilled cybersecurity professionals across industries.
  • Impactful Work: Contribution to protecting sensitive data and maintaining privacy.
  • Technological Innovation: Involvement in developing and implementing advanced security technologies.
  • Versatile Career Paths: Opportunities to work in diverse sectors and roles.
  • Career Growth: Potential for specialization and advancement in a rapidly evolving field.
